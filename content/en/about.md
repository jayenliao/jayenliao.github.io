I am a Master's student in Computer Science at National Taiwan University (NTU), advised by Prof. Shang-Tse Chen, where I work on trustworthy AI — the fairness of graph neural networks and the robustness of large language models.

I care about whether a model is not only accurate but also fair, robust, and trustworthy — especially for the people most likely to be overlooked when systems fail. My background spans Statistics, Psychology, Data Science, and Computer Science, and before returning to research I spent a year as a Data Scientist at Fazz, a Singaporean FinTech startup, building fraud- and anomaly-detection systems on real-world financial data.

I'm currently open to research collaborations and internships in AI / ML — feel free to [reach out](mailto:jay.chiehen@gmail.com).
